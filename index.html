A Cover Worth 1000 Words


how aesthetic features can influence your purchases

Aesthetics tend to be taken for granted. Who cares if something looks good? Function before style, right? While there is some truth to that, visual appeal definitely plays an important role in our everyday lives. Aesthetics can affect everything from how we feel, to the decisions that we make - sometimes without us even realizing it!
Often when we think ‘aesthetics’, the first things that come to mind are probably envy-enticing photos that flood Instagram and Tumblr. However, aesthetic is used in the creation of books, movies, and even music, and is an overarching term that includes everything from typography to delivery. Arguably, it’s these more basic building blocks that can have the biggest influence on the human psyche. Whether you’re a customer or a product designer, learning more about aesthetics can really help you to make savvier decisions. Not convinced? Understandable - it can be a tough concept to wrap your head around, and your first instinct might be to click out of this page entirely. Let’s see if we can change your mind though.
We’ve used data from zygmuntz’s goodbooks dataset (a compilation of data - genre, ratings, etc.- of various books) from the book-rating site, goodreads) as well as information gathered by running book cover images through Google’s Vision API (using their “scores” result) to figure out the dominant colors and symbols used on different book covers) in order to create the informative visualizations on this website. Hopefully they can help to provide some insite into this cool topic. Before we dive in, though, let’s first begin with a quick example.

Imagine this scene: you walk into a bookstore and immediately there’s a book that catches your eye and draws your attention. Which one is it?
SYMBOL VIEW

Just as important as color, are the symbols that appear on book covers. In this visualization, the bar chart to the left shows the most popular symbols that are used on book covers, grouped by genre. The tree map to the right shows the main color of the books within that genre. Feel free to use the drop-down box to filter by a specific genre.
